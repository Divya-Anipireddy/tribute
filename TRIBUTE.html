ABDUL KALAM

Dr.A.P.J.Abdul Kalam
11th President of India
Born:15 October 1931, Rameshwaram.
Died:27 July 2015 (age 83 years), Shillong.
Education:Madras Institute of Technology, Anna University (1955–1957).

Missile Man of India

Kalam was the youngest of five siblings, the eldest of whom was a sister, Asim Zohra (d. 1997), followed by three elder brothers: Mohammed Muthu Meera Lebbai Maraikayar (5 November 1916 – 7 March 2021),Mustafa Kalam (d. 1999) and Kasim Mohammed (d. 1995). He was extremely close to his elder siblings and their extended families throughout his life, and would regularly send small sums of money to his older relations, himself remaining a lifelong bachelor.

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam was one of India’s most celebrated Presidents. He was known for his dedication to the country and his efforts to improve the lives of its citizens. Dr. Kalam served as President from 2002-2007, during which time he made significant contributions to India’s economic and social development.
Under Dr. Kalam’s leadership, India made great strides in improving its infrastructure and boosting its economy. He also helped to improve the education system and expand access to healthcare. Dr. Kalam was a strong advocate for women’s rights and worked to empower women across India.

During his Presidency, Dr. Kalam earned the respect of people all over the world for his integrity and commitment to peace. He was a man of great vision and inspired many with his passion for progress. Dr. Kalam will always be remembered as one of India’s greatest leaders.
